Carving Legend

Heroes – men and women who defy the mortal realm.
Their deeds, that are seemingly of the impossible
Are achieved by the might of their skill:
The thunder of raving swords, with blood thumping applause,
The clashing of otherworldly foes to awaken any god’s slumber.
By the pain of their blood, the stress of their sweat,
And by the humanly tears that would make any water weep,
They do the seemingly impossible, and in return they
Step into the mortal realm with their glory, fame, and honor.

What was I in comparison to these mortal gods?
A lowly solider I once was, good on the battlefield I may be,
Will never compare to the likes of those giants.
The titans made in human form,
The ones who have the ears of the gods,
For they are blessed and cursed to forevermore
Be the ones reaching into the likes of divinity.

How can I be a hero? How can I be like them?
Those questions would oft be on my mind
When I continue the wary path,
Moving from town to town, city to city,
After my younger days living the life of a warrior,
Seeking my glory, and if the gods demand it,
A great death on the battlefield.
So, when it was that one day under the auspice of night,
With the full moon up above, nor any clouds obstructing,
That I find a narrow field between the mighty forest trees.
I began to set up the camp, with the campfire running tinder aflame,
The surrounding ambiance making a mockery
Of its daylight counterpart from the crickets chirping,
The soothing quiet akin to peaceful spirits.
If this is what the Elysium Fields look like in the afterlife,
Then when I die, I hope to find my eternal rest there.

But sadly, while the rest of the world goes quietly by
For me my senses alerted me to the chill in the air,
With the sweat on the back and the fear apparent,
I knew by the state of the much too quiet silence
That there is something hiding in the woods.

I kept to my relaxed position
And a false calming façade,
But I knew that whatever
Is stalking the woods at this time of night,
Where the full moon shines with no clouds overhead,
The stars shining brightly onto the worldly visage,
That whatever is out there is no human.
How I knew that I could never say,
But worse than the greatest terror I had ever felt
I now experienced worse at this present moment.

At that moment, where time seemed immemorial,
Came a beast shall forevermore be burned into my memory:
A beast of lion make, but unlike any ordinary lion.
I rolled out of the way, with sword in my hand
Facing the mythical lion with dread but steadfast courage.
I knew of the fate that most likely await me,
But until such a time happens,
The Underworld will not take me this day!

So, with a warlike scream I charged,
The lion returned with claws enlarged,
Its roar from its maw making way
For what undoubtedly would be its prey,
I strike with caution; the lions claws landing late,
A sword strike is earned, starting the test of fate.
The lion roared to the heavens, its intentions clear,
My greatest battle I charged again overcoming my fear,
The lion raced and jumped quick like a hare,
Barely just reaching the breadth of my hair,
I slide underneath, the lions back turned,
A slash of blood I gave and earned,
But in return its quick nature launched an attack,
Of which landed me on my back.
My sword got knocked out from me,
And my chest is blooded, its claw pattern evident to see,
The lion slowly stalking, see that its prey is down,
But don’t expect me to be down for the count!

And so, I pretended to be weak, my breath on fire,
My veins wrought with fury most dire.
Like heroes of old, this lion will earn my ire,
And shall go down over gods inspire!

When the lion came close to launch the killing blow,
I rolled like silent lighting, with sword in hand.
Raise my sword in a horizontal position
Against the base of its neck,
Over which its blood detached dead.

Now like the rest of the world it becomes silent.
My breath is struggling, my chest in pain,
The battle-high of survival and fear wearing off.
I can see the rising dawn coming over the forest walls,
And to become the deed of my trophy I took the lion’s head.
Beheaded from its bodily state I went to continue
The trek to my ancestral home.

Thinking now, am I a hero?
I who did the heroic deed of slaying a beast.
Me who someone overcome a seemingly impossible
Task fit for heroes and gods?

In the town of my people, when they look at the lion’s head,
They stand silent at the man who did the impossible mortal stead.

Eyes Upon Me

Creep in this hallow woods, with trees of seemingly eyes,
They track and seek and see all that happens:
Do they speak like humans do, or do they speak silently?
The judgmental look we should feel; of passing shame
But nothing does the wicked way come when one becomes lost.

I feel like everything is, but no one is watching me.
The crows and ravens, speaking nevermore,
Thus kindly I speak with craws and knows,
The million fireflies lighting, sparking, with buzzing passerby.
The grass and leaves whistling like shaking thunder,
The crickets humming with frequent passion.
I stand on top of dirt road, untraveled and unwarded
Through the supernatural I fear has come:
I see nor spy the watch upon my visage,
But feel the same that are eyes upon me.

Do the trees see, or the fear I think is messing with my mind?
Bristle with the nightly branches; nightly full moon overhead.
And out of the fright of the weeping silence a howl arose.
Shadows moving about, too quick for the average eyes:
Something is here, bipedal in nature… no, it could not be.

A noise behind me, a noise of the left… then the right.
All times I checked, and saw with a high certainty:
A werewolf.

And the howling comes nearer and comes more in nature.

A scream, and rage of immense aura I notice,
Even at this distance?

And out comes the beast in question, no doubt I see,
The trees, they are most likely crackling at my perceived slight:
Whether it be on my honor or at the time of my impending doom
Know I do not the true preciosity of the eyes.

The werewolf is in front of me, and as if on timely queue,
The overhead full moon shines brightly onto the beastly visage,
The fur, though black, strikes softly and smooth.
The head, with full muzzle, open with sharp teeth,
And the arms elongated with killing claws.

The werewolf stopped, only looking at me.
I stared back as well, too frighten to make any sudden moves.

Though the eyes of the werewolf, I can see with no mistake:
The werewolf, while seemingly stereotypical of beastly nature,
Is intelligent. I suspect this werewolf is a human.
A wolf-shifter? And how does the world shatter when faced with the new?
Or dare I say old, with the mythological nature of a werewolf?
Supernatural I never saw before, but now a true believer:
The werewolf, in its eyes, are filled with rage.

In death, I suppose, my time has come.
I shut my eyes.

Then seconds’ pass. I heard the sound of trampled grass,
And when nothing occurs onto my person,
I open my eyes only to see the werewolf gone.
And seemingly of the like-nature disappearance
Like the confoundment evidence of a werewolf never even here.

Was the werewolf a figment of my imagination…?
No. The werewolf was too real in nature.
I saw it. I do not doubt.
Nothing is here now, and with no evidence to prove my claim.
But I know… and this werewolf, maybe we will
See each other someday, but for now…

I will track my way throughout the light,
The lost day I feared will end in fright:
Through the windows of the eyes
That trek into the soul, whichever so happens
That an encounter like mind:
Go to at the end of the path,
And see the civilization I left behind.
