Where My Worries Shall Go

Oh where, oh where
My worries shall go?
I do not know where my
Worries shall go. Here? There?
Across the meadow into the overland?
Or maybe just a quaint little town
Where everyone shall know you:
Your names, personality, and oh,
How does the time go by!
Or maybe a forest, green and lush,
Surrounded by endless brush,
And bushes and sticks; tall trees,
Covering, blocking for passage view?
Or maybe the ocean, and its
Endless sea, stretching out as
Far as the eye can see; or on a
Sailor-boat, greetin’ the captain
And the members of her crew:
Unbound, endless, free, or maybe
It’s a lie about the truth
That death can be eternal.
Or maybe it’s myself, sitting
And looking up to the night sky,
Sitting all by my lonesome
Tired and restless; or maybe
The dawn sea breaks up
And shines onto me and everything
I worry, I see, or do.
So, I don’t know where my
Worries shall go, and but I do know
Worries come and go, all from time to time;
But it is the future only looking
Forward too, and keeping current
Tides on high and low.
If only, if only if I did ever know.

"When you look at me and my heart"

When you look at me and my heart, it’s when I shed and cry:
The tears I weep and show to you; your death seemed like a dream;
I would never ever think, not once in a million years, for you to die:
If this reality was the all basis of hell, then I wished it not to seem.

The falsage breaks, and how I would remember all your dastardly conversations:
O, it seemed like the good times would never end,
And it would feel that bondage of us is divine sent; of opposite revelations.
You were such a friend; you were such a godsend…

To I! But it is only me still standing here to cry and eternal torment! —
Right now, all I want to do right now is to curse the world;
This world is only deserving in its hatred. But death laid dormant,
And awaited… Yes, awaited for me as if things unfurled.

Were it was I to die in your stead, in your place, instead of you,
Just I wonder what the world would behold to you:
The mysteries of your mind, the time stopped; only of what I knew…
But it’s only I being here to weep and cry, oh whatever shall I do?

The White Burless

Twinkle amidst the nightful stars that winters’ tales
Spring to life from the opera that mourns
Between the hours of sun and half-past noon
To the sounds of burgundy on footprint trace
On the side of covered snow down sidewalk
By the streets pass when people gone and fro;
Then to be blocked of passerby’s and cars gleam
Of sirens matrix doubt ringed interred by our ears:
And nightly whispers near the signposts do ream
By the time we make do with each of our fears.

The melody by remembrance of violins that play
Over the seas and view many times out mark
To the eyes elsed known close to the body
Of The White Burless. With eyes so pure,
But embodied posed with heartfelt rage
Entombed the muscle memory and strike
With hefty force there all she wrote.

From immemorial mankind with safety paramount
Taken with assuming jokes of clash vanities
Imtember with the lovely song from above
To white doves singing with the hearts of love
Ever remembering our days with The White Burless.

Upon the Sacred Night

And what shall cause the midnight dance upon the sacred night;
Where turmoil and bright shall burn away with the midnight stars?
Though the elder greens of pastor colors shining under the glowing light;
Then nothingness of love and tenderness with hugs and tender flair:
Whence time seems never-ending — when the feeling becomes ever-nice.
Then pray that the happy dream comes the morning after,
Under the arms of those too close to wield that we can cry and hold dear.
Though to some on the lonely path dare none made forth to take:
That of the curse of the loneliness of all;
And say upon those with love to care: who cares for the ones who’s alone?
Else upon the midnight dance — upon the sacred night.

The Coordinates Eye

O, what I’ll give for the Coordinates Eye!
What fairer takes for foreign land,
And to see the sea and the sand,
Noticing out the dreams of common man;
Yet you noticed, so you reached out your hand
And you sought the things you didn’t know,
For winter comes, and whiteness snow.
From that frost is a hellish gaze
That give brutal monsters a stone-cold face,
And voyages beyond the grove of forgotten land,
As you dreamed about the warming sand;
But nothing the eye can deceive to disgrace,
And give false barrow for the Coordinates Eye.

O, I shall tell you of the seasons,
How they roam and soar the sky.
But a golden harp told a lie,
And I could tell your beauteous face
Was disgusted for me; and what I didn’t know
I once asked to forgive: O, I did ask,
And pleaded! And begged!
But no, I’ll never think again about you:
For you are a liar, a deceiver;
A cheating scar of a leaver.
So I ask you to be gone,
And to leave this place,
And forever not to return.

The Coordinates Eye I saw from horror
In distant dreams to see frozen scraps,
And blood and scars; even a golden mace,
Like a momentous horse running a race,
Outdoing the rest, forwarding the pace,
But enough of that!
The Coordinates Eye I saw and seen,
From the distant Heaven from my dream,
Saw myself, then spoke unto me:
“O, wise and fortunate Captain, the seas forbids;
Therefore forever waters that stricken
The tremendous gold that you possess.
Your carriage I ask to come to sand
To seek out the distant, foreign land.
Paradise awaits for you, O Captain;
Go seek out the light.
No more fairing in the night.
Bask for what is the greatest, the best,
The most perfect; now seek out this test,
And I give you farewell and Fortune,
From my Coordinates Eye.”

I then awoke from my dream,
Paradise then seemed like a theme;
A test for me to forego,
What future and misfortune I do not know.
But, winter… winter I must go.
Autumn, I loved and know.
Spring, what bounty you can give,
Summer, you give us a will to live.
Paradise, to redeem myself from sin;
From the least of me so I can win.
The journey I got from the Coordinates Eye,
So from the mortal world I’ll say goodbye,
But first before I go,
I want to tell my beauty to the world:

O, the world I see out in the night sky,
The sky would’ve told you, “I love you.”
The animals and nomads of the land
Gives life from your hand.
Therefore I think of everything:
The color blue, and bells rung,
From the voices of my song.
From the Coordinates Eye
I, at last, say my goodbye
So I’ll go up to Paradise.

Winter

If there was one thing in the world I could see joyfully,
It would be winter, and how her cold and her snow
Would place themselves over me; and they would cover
Me in white, as else with the rest of the world.
By glaze I saw in sleep I then became alive;
If I ever was alive. But maybe I could call out,
And ask winter how you feel and how…
How the winter doth feels about me.

I could rest by a tree and see it shallow,
Where the birds sing it all the more hollow,
And to see the leaves fall frozen stiff,
Clueless; wandering by them all: why ever do they fall?
I could continue on about the other things,
But all things arrive at one: once things born
Now was withering and decaying beyond the scope
Of things beyond. And by that statement bores a question:
If I do know an answer, may I keep myself to it all?
If there was one thing in the world I could see joyfully,
It would be to see the winter. But beyond the grave I couldn’t
See what I could become; so I was amorphous,
But I could hear all the rude-ish judgement
From the mouths of many from the world I left.
If you all had at least once think that I was a fool,
A bastard, an evil man, a freak;
But I could see from my eye
All your spoken thoughts of truths and eyes.
If I could speak and confront your confoundment,
(And dare I say, the shock off your faces,)
All I have to ask from you all is an honest conversation;
And I know hatred is enviable, and some will misunderstand,
And some will think less and judge from
The deepest regions of your heart;
But I go on to converse:
“If there was one thing in the world...
One thing joyfully…
It would be winter,
And her winter snow.”
And all those voices would reply back:
“Why only one thing in the world?
And why her winter snow?
Do doth shape and curl,
In death, to let it go?”
And I would think, and think, and think.
Was death an excuse of me for my hatred?
And what is winter to me?
Does she not revolve around me?
“Does not,” I spoke aloud,
“The winter belong to me?”
After I spoke, those people kneeled upon me,
And chanted a verse of holy poetry.
(O, the irony!)
“We pray to you and give grace;
Please no more death upon this place.
We ask of you to be our light,
To fairer be-gone unholy night.
Sacred, sacred, holy bless,
Love us at our best!
We are sinners, far and wide,
So give us as our guide.
And to take and be aside,
We’ll serve you in death and life.
So give grace to us and perform
The basis of holy miracles
That hovers like a storm;
Your lasting mysticals
We say, ‘Our lord and grace.’”

To hear that as they praised that chant,
I had to admit to myself: I felt something,
An emotion of guilt and hope, something…
Something I never felt before,
Not ever in my thousands of years of being.
No… my beginning of time as the beholder of winter.

If there was one thing in the world I could see joyfully,
Would be my winter, and how her winter snow falls.
A contrast of Autumn.

O, I give the one thing the world has joyfully:
The winter, and her great winter snow.

"My light, my muse, shining so bright;"

My light, my muse, shining so bright;
A star that beams upon the night,
So fair and tender on your skin so white:
My love to you makes me sing delight.

Your grace, your love, there so sacred on,
That I hope with your gaze keeps me upon; -
Nothing compares – nor riches, fame, or glory,
For yourself makes itself into legendary story.

But nothing your supple mouth, and beauteous eyes:
Bluer than that makes the daylight sky,
I speak, but softly, thus my heart:
Beating so heavily your sight gives it start;

I confess my love, purer than the spring,
And may many joys will happily bring.

"Inside the rainstorm asunder that went on forever,"

Inside the rainstorm asundered that went on forever,
The cries of one could be heard,
Inside a cabin that two men roam, but encountered Death's dome,
And the blood of dove's and birds.

Alive, but barely weakening one man cried,
And the other with his weaponry out:
The monsters of the night, they surround in smoky sight;
Games be played - one winner that be proud.

Deception of choice will one make - to live for one's sake!
To give up the past of his friend,
Who is one that monsters will choke, thus hardened this bloke.
To be or not to be; if one ends up dead.

Hours and such minutes has passed, never thought they'll last:
The monsters, they came,
And the fought between two, never though monsters would knew;
The lives - of monsters - they claimed.

Knifes chipped and bullet rung, empathy did Death's choir sung.
Sunrise may be soon away,
But not enough for savior or miracle, for cries were seen lyrical:
Blood and grasp has claimed his last day.

The only survivor man had stand, his firm grasped, and looked at the land,
He walked out, the body laid;
He never looked back, only then he sighed and let his wrath attacked:
The monsters had something to fear this day.

And when it finally ended, Heavens and Hell be splendid,
The man looked and saw the sky:
His look mesmerizing, but underneath his expression despising.
Why did his friend, who he loved, died and lied?

"As once upon a night so dreary,"

As once upon a night so dreary,
Wondering, thinking, with thoughts so weary:
So bright it seems, my contemporaries, in brilliancy;
The buoyant of simple and inspiring cause
That with floating of heights so inured to reach,
It towers upon the heavens and beyond,
Where of average and normal souls
May hope to dream and look upon – to gaze
Upwards to the bright sky, and reach the stars
To discover each and every one
Of its undiscovered stories.
This is where we dream
Of a journey that is bring’d thusly so:
A time of adventure; of unsung and born heroes,
Wielding a might against the gaze of unhourly abyss.
A mind with a dream – a mind to invent a story so brilliant
Brings a wonderment to all of mankind
Against our own fallacy, an example of raising from the ashes.
So, weep not against the eye of the storm,
Breaking those of ironclad will.
We will fall, but we will fall with a purpose,
And return stronger than ever.
It is a reminder to dream of a story
Filled with all those young, childlike wonders
Pacing with steady steps to a world
Ready for such stars.
And with a rest on a night so dreary,
As sun may rise to future anew:
Sing a toast to a melody most merry,
And let loose the brilliance within you.

A Clasping Dream

If to be the hanger of a clasping dream,
Do I wonder when I will sleep;
Or else would I have to awake and work,
But when escaping reality is only a snooze away,
I’ll just have to wait, because it’s here
Where I become a new man, a better man
Then I was the day before; and this is where
I don’t have to wonder about the pain.
Only but a clasping dream: a wholeful’s
Telling inside a storybook, to see the things
Regular life cannot. This is why I dream,
And why for dream’s sake will I continue,
Either in sleep or in day; out of reality,
If to be the hanger of a clasping dream.
